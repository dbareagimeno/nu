Log of everything that has been decided **not to decide yet**. Each entry
has a *trigger*: the signal indicating it's time to reopen it. Nothing on this
list is rejected; it's waiting for its moment. When an entry is
reopened and decided, it leaves here and enters the ADR.

**Deferred implementation (P22–P31).** A second group of entries isn't waiting for a
*decision* but for a *build*: their design already lives in their extension's
contract (`agente.md`, `chat.md`, `providers.md`), but the first version of the
official extensions (`0.1.0`, sessions S36–S45) left them out of scope. The
core of each extension works; what's deferred is a concrete layer. When reopened
they don't go to the ADR —they're already decided— but to a build session; their
*trigger* signals when it's worth building them. In the meantime, the contract points
here at the affected signature so no one assumes them present.

## Footnote: P21 — DECIDED (ADR-016 / G34)

P21 was the **only** entry in this batch that wasn't a build but a
**canonical-model decision** (`providers.md` §2.1): changing a contract
signature is decided explicitly, not by an adapter's fait accompli. That's
why it was reserved. It has now been **reopened and resolved** by the project's
design flow: validated with pseudocode ([Round 7, scenario 32](pseudocodigo.md)),
registered as crack [G34](problemas.md#g34) and decided in
[ADR-016](adr.md#adr-016--modelo-canónico-de-thinking-con-mode-y-traducción-por-modelo-en-el-adaptador).
It leaves postponed items; only its build remains.

**The problem.** The canonical model froze `thinking?: { budget?: integer }` and the
`anthropic` adapter translated it to the *legacy* form `{type="enabled",
budget_tokens=N}`. The Opus 4.6+ family (incl. the default model,
`claude-opus-4-8`) **removed `budget_tokens`** and expects `{type:"adaptive"}`: a
request with `thinking.budget` on those models gets **400**. It was a crack in the
**canonical model** (it lacked a way to express adaptive mode and the data on which
form each model understands), not in the adapter. It was **latent** (the agent doesn't populate
`req.thinking` by default), but its trigger had already been met.

**The decision (ADR-016).** Two pieces:

1. **Canonical model, by addition** → `thinking?: { mode?: "off"|"adaptive"|"budget",
   budget?: integer }`. `thinking` absent = no reasoning; `{budget=N}` without
   `mode` = a compatible alias for `mode="budget"` (doesn't break the frozen signature).
2. **Each model's dialect is DATA** in `providers.toml`
   (`thinking = "adaptive"|"budget"|"none"`, default `"budget"`), which the adapter
   reads to translate **per-model** —`adaptive`→`{type="adaptive"}`,
   `budget`→`{type="enabled", budget_tokens=N}`, degrading between the two depending on the
   dialect—. The adapter doesn't hardcode model-version tables
   (ADR-003/ADR-005); the per-model-id heuristic was discarded.

**Status: IMPLEMENTED.** After being decided in the ADR, the build session
applied the contract: `thinking_to_wire` in `adapter_anthropic.lua` translates the
canonical `thinking` per the model's dialect, `resolve` carries `model.thinking` into the
`ModelInfo`, and `providers_p21_test.go` covers all eight combinations (dialect ×
mode). The only thing missing is **wiring the reasoning control from the
agent** (today `req.thinking` is only populated by a `request.pre` hook): when the
agent exposes that option, it will map to `thinking` — but the contract and adapter
already support it.
